Daraz's Guide to the World
The Statue's Song
                            Oh sing to me ere all is lost,
                            of memories the flesh still bears;
                            recount to me how our paths cross’d,
                            face to face, amid the moss.

                            Hidden in the whisp’ring woods,
                            this ember heart met icy cold.
                            Oh! the chill that I withstood,
                            skin to steam, it hurt so good.

                            Now tempered to each other’s blood,
                            our skins have formed to husks of stone.
                            Forever frozen ‘twixt the buds;
                            self to statue, mired in mud.

                            The moss has risen, blocked the light;
                            its roots dig deep below my skin,
                            forming cracks I cannot fight;
                            stone to dust, my love; goodnight.
